## Next (post-water)

- [x] Ship on lit meshes — each hull piece is a lit box (per-face N·L + ambient) on the shader pipeline; debugdraw removed entirely (water + ship are both GPU now). `src/ship_mesh.*`, `shaders/{vs,fs}_mesh.sc`.
- [x] Interactive cargo/damage — the ship is live: a cargo slider + Damage/Repair/Reset buttons + keys (C/V/X/Z/R) feed straight into buoyancy. Overloading or breaking planks drops the float margin, the ship rides lower, then **founders and sinks beneath the waves** (progressive sink accumulation); lighten it or repair in time and it recovers. Panel shows live status (afloat / SINKING), cargo, damage, float margin.
- [x] Sailing (Black Flag control scheme) — W/S step sail tiers (anchored / half / full), A/D steer (more agile at low speed), chase camera behind the heading. Ship-centric: the hull stays at the origin and the ocean scrolls past (wave field sampled at the ship's virtual world position, so buoyancy stays consistent). See [references/black-flag.md](references/black-flag.md).
- [x] Wind + visible sail (Black Flag style) — a wind direction drifts over time; you make best speed running with it (downwind → 100% drive) and least beating into it (upwind → ~55%). Panel shows the wind bearing + tail/cross/head + drive %. A mast + canvas sail now render on the hull (data-driven on the ship's mast/sail counts) and the sail trims partway toward the wind.
- [x] Black Flag control scheme, matched to the researched original — see [references/black-flag.md](references/black-flag.md#sailing-controls-researched-matched-in-the-native-prototype). Four discrete sail states (**anchored / half sail / full sail / travel speed**); **half sail is the spawn/cruise baseline**; **W** taps sail up and **holding W at full latches travel speed** (the tap-vs-hold split); **S** steps down and coasts to a stop with **no reverse**; **turn radius inversely coupled to speed** (~77°/s pivot at a dead stop vs ~48°/s at travel — the stop-to-turn tactic, verified live); the **visible sail reefs with the state** (furled → half → full). Wind-gating kept as a deliberate enhancement over BF's wind-neutral model.
- [x] Naval gunnery — **Space fires a broadside** at a drifting target ship. Cannonballs (one per cannon, spread along the hull, lobbed from whichever side faces the target) arc under gravity and, on hitting the target's hull box, flood its planks — so the target progressively **founders and sinks**, reusing the existing buoyancy/damage/sink loop. Projectile/hit/damage logic lives in the pure-C++ model spine (`fireBroadside`/`stepProjectiles`/`resolveHits`/`pointInHull`) with **6 self-tests (18/18 total)**. Renders the target hull + tracer cannonballs at world-relative positions.
- [x] Two-sided naval battle — the target is now an **AI warship** (`aiCaptain` in the model spine): it crowds sail to close, turns to present a broadside, and **fires back**. Its cannonballs (red tracers) flood *your* hull, so you can be sunk too — a real duel with a **win/lose** outcome (VICTORY when the enemy founders, DEFEAT if you do). 6 more self-tests for the AI decision logic (**24/24 total**). Panel shows enemy status/damage, range, and incoming shots.
- [x] Boarding (the Black Flag crown jewel) — cripple the enemy until it floods to the waterline and **strikes her colours** (stops fighting, furls sail, coasts to a dead stop). Pull **alongside** (< 12 m) at low speed and press **B** to **board & capture** her before she founders — a distinct **VICTORY - captured** outcome vs. just sinking her. Melee-on-deck is abstracted to a short boarding beat for now (next: the real on-deck fight).
- [x] Real sailing feel & look — replaced the arcade wind model with true **points of sail** (`sailPower` polar in the model spine): a **no-go zone** near the wind where the sails luff and there's *no drive* (you must **tack** upwind), fastest on a **reach**, slower dead downwind. The hull now **heels to leeward** under sail pressure (most on a reach), sails **trim** from athwartships (running) to fore-and-aft (close-hauled) and **luff/shiver** in irons. Panel shows the point of sail + drive %, with an "in irons - bear away!" warning. 4 more self-tests (**28/28**); live-confirmed heel + trim + drive tracking (58% close-hauled → 75% running → 84% close reach).
- [x] Wind vane HUD + apparent wind — a masthead vane (top-right): bow-up compass with the **no-go zone** (red) and best-**reach** arcs (green), a **true** (blue) and **apparent** (amber) wind arrow, and two **telltales** that stream aft when the sail draws and flutter when it luffs, with an "IN IRONS - bear away" warning. Apparent wind (`apparentWind` in the model spine, self-tested — the true wind combined with the boat's own motion; it shifts forward and strengthens as you sail) drives the amber arrow. Custom-drawn with the ImGui draw list. 29/29 self-tests; live-confirmed.
- [x] **Build mode v0 (guided assembly)** — grounded in real shipbuilding history ([references/shipbuilding-history.md](references/shipbuilding-history.md), from a verified deep-research pass). A `BuildTradition` (Roman / Viking / English) with `buildOrder` in the model spine that places pieces in the historically-correct sequence — **shell-first** (Roman/Viking: planks before frames) vs **frame-first** (English: skeleton before planking). A "Build mode" panel freezes sailing/combat and reveals the hull **piece by piece** (Lay next / Strike last / Build all / Reveal slider), showing each tradition's step-by-step build list with the current step highlighted. 4 self-tests (**33/33**); live-confirmed (English frame-first shows the keel+frames skeleton before any planking). This is the first half of the **Build → Sail** loop.
- [x] Backbone pieces — the ship model now generates the real **backbone the player makes**: `keel`, `stem` (bow post, raked forward), `sternpost` (stern post), and the `rib`s. `buildOrder` lays the whole backbone first (keel → stem → sternpost) before any framing/planking, in every tradition; the posts render as tall raked timbers at bow and stern (distinct backbone color). 2 self-tests; live-confirmed in Build mode.
- [x] Rig, spars, fittings & lines — the ship is now built out **all the way**: **planking** (strakes) + **fastenings** (the treenail/rivet "nails" step in the sequence), the **deck**, a **mast** and **bowsprit** (pine spars), **fittings** (helm wheel + capstan), and the **standing rigging** — shrouds + fore/back **stays**, the "lines" — all as real pieces laid in the correct order (backbone → planking → nails → deck → mast/bowsprit → fit-out → rigging). Spars/fittings/lines carry tiny volumes so they don't distort the crude float model (the `HullProfile` bake is the real hydrostatics). Live-confirmed: the boat now sails fully rigged. **44/44** self-tests.
- [x] Shipyard **build berth + stand** — Build mode happens AT the island's shipyard: the hull sits up on a **stand** (ground ways + keel blocks + shoring posts) on the dock, with a ship hall + gantry cranes behind, under a slow **orbit camera** so you can look it over from every side.
- [x] **Hull profile bake (Build → Sail bridge)** — **shape the hull** (length/beam/depth sliders), **submit for launch** (a "calculating…" beat), and it **bakes a `HullProfile`** (displacement, block coeff, GM stability, top-speed & turn factors) from the shape. **Sailing reads the profile**, so a fine hull is fast & wide-turning while a beamy hull is slow, stiff & tight-turning — no two boats sail the same. `bakeHullProfile` in the model spine, 5 self-tests (**43/43**); live-confirmed. See [build-mode.md](build-mode.md#implementation-direction-native-prototype).

Then the bake reads the **actual placed geometry**, not just L/B/depth sliders. Plus the rest of the taxonomy (garboard, wales, floors, futtocks, keelson, deck beams, knees) and clinker-vs-carvel plank visuals.
- [x] **Walkable human character (v0)** — a third-person box-figure human (head/torso/legs/arms, animated stride) you drive with **WASD** (A/D turn) around the shipyard, with a follow camera; walk the yard around and under your ship on the stocks. Toggled from Build mode ("Walk the yard"). `ship_mesh::renderCharacter` + a follow-cam branch in `renderBuildScene`. Live-confirmed.

- [ ] **Pulleys & ropes** — block-and-tackle for the yard cranes (hoisting timbers/masts) and the ship's rigging; rope as a constraint/catenary, pulley as a hoist ratio. Big.
- [x] **Island + port + large shipyard, with harbour mechanics** — a large island (beach / grass / hill / rocky peak) with a working **port** (three piers, stone quay, warehouses, harbourmaster tower, lighthouse, crates, a moored boat) and a **large shipyard** (great ship hall, three slipways, gantry cranes, scaffolding, timber stacks, and a **half-built hull on the stocks**) sits at a fixed spot you sail to (the ocean grid was widened so it reads from a distance). **Collision** (`keepOutsideCircle` in the model spine, self-tested) runs the hull aground at the shore instead of sailing through the island; the grounding penalty is edge-triggered so you're not glued to the beach. First real **world content** — the anchor for a proper Build → Sail loop (build at the shipyard, sail out).
